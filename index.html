What High-Ticket Is For

High-ticket is not a more expensive version of mid-ticket. It is a fundamentally different relationship — deeper, more personal, and built around custom outcomes that only you can deliver.

◆
Maximum personalisationEvery client's journey is tailored. No templates, no group calls as the main delivery. You are the product.
◆
Premium price = premium commitmentA high-ticket buyer who invested $3k–$10k shows up. Low-ticket buyers sometimes don't. Price filters for action-takers.
◆
Application required — alwaysNot everyone qualifies. The application process is not gatekeeping — it's quality control that protects your results and reputation.
◆
The proof machine for your brandOne high-ticket client who gets a transformative result is worth 50 low-ticket testimonials. This is where case studies are made.
◆
Price range: $2,000 – $10,000+Weekly 1:1 calls. Private support. Custom guidance. Price reflects the depth of access and the weight of results.

01Offer Design
Designing the Offer

Four non-negotiables every high-ticket offer must have before it goes to market. These four elements also become the foundation of your positioning, scripts, and sales call.

◆
TransformationFrom [current situation] to [dreamed outcome]. Specific, vivid, and measurable. Not "better results" — "from 0 to $5k/month in 90 days."
◆
TimeframeDefined duration creates urgency and sets expectations. 3 months, 6 months, or 12 months. Open-ended engagements drift.
◆
Method (your unique process)What makes your approach different? This is what justifies the premium price. Name it if you can — a named method is more memorable and sellable.
◆
Constraints — who it's NOT forThe most powerful positioning tool you have. Saying who you won't work with makes the right person feel more chosen.
◆
These four elements are not just offer design — they become the foundation of your VSL, your DM scripts, your sales call opening, and your Typeform qualifier. Define them once. Use them everywhere.

01BPricing Model
Fixed vs Flexible Pricing

The pricing model you choose changes the entire sales process. Choose based on offer complexity and buyer readiness — not on what feels comfortable.

Model A
Fixed Price
No sales call required
Typeform qualifier + direct to checkout
Works for offers under $3,000
Self-qualified buyers — higher intent
Faster path to revenue
Best for: productised high-ticket with a clear, defined outcome
VS
Model B · Most Common
Flexible / Call-Based
Sales call required
Price discussed and closed on the call
Works for offers $3,000–$10,000+
Allows custom scoping per client
Higher close rate with right script
Best for: bespoke engagements where scope varies per client
◆
Fixed pricing removes friction and scales faster. Call-based pricing allows you to tailor scope and price — but requires a trained sales process. Neither is better. The right one depends on your offer.

02Positioning
Positioning the Offer

Positioning is not what you say about yourself. It's what your ideal client believes about you before they ever speak to you. These three questions must be answered with specificity — not left open.

◆
Who is this for, exactly?Not "coaches and creators." Specifically: what niche, what revenue stage, what platform, what problem are they currently stuck at? The more specific, the more magnetic.
◆
What problem stage are they at?Are they starting from zero, stuck at $1–3k/mo, or scaling past $5k? The problem stage determines your message, your entry point, and your price.
◆
Why you, and not someone else?Your lived experience, your results, your method. This is not modesty territory — it's the answer to the objection they're already thinking. Write it down before the first sales call.
◆
These three answers go directly into your VSL script, your DM opener, and your sales call introduction. Positioning is not a marketing exercise — it is a sales preparation exercise.

The Entry System

The entry system does two jobs: attract the right people and filter out the wrong ones before they reach the sales call. Every unqualified call is a wasted hour.

01
Content
Authority content with DM trigger (e.g. "DM MENTOR")
02
DM / Funnel
Automated or manual DM sequence opens the conversation
03
Qualification
One question filters fit. Low quality leads → auto-nurture
04
Book Call
Qualified leads book via Calendly. CRM captures all data.
◆
The qualification questionOne question that surfaces budget, commitment, and fit. Example: "What's your current monthly revenue and what's stopping you from 3× it?" Disqualifies by answer, not by gatekeeping.
◆
Auto-nurture for not-ready leadsLeads who don't qualify now are not lost — they go into an email + broadcast sequence. They become buyers in 30–90 days. Never discard a lead who showed intent.
◆
CRM follow-up systemEvery qualified lead who books is tracked. No-shows are followed up. Every touchpoint is logged. The CRM is the nervous system of the entry system.

04BSales — Critical Additions
The Three Scenarios That Kill Close Rates

These three scenarios account for 80% of lost deals. Every operator needs a scripted response for each — not improvised, not hoped away. Written and rehearsed before the first call.

"I need to think about it."
"Totally understand. What specifically do you need to think through? Is it the investment, the timing, or whether this is the right fit?" — Identify the real objection. Then address that, not the surface one.
"I can't afford it right now."
"If money wasn't the issue, would this be the right solution for you?" If yes: explore payment plans. If no: the money objection is hiding a different objection. Find it.
No-show on the call.
Follow-up 1: within 15 mins. Follow-up 2: next day with a rebook link. Follow-up 3: 3 days later, final attempt. After three no-responses: move to nurture sequence, not the bin.
◆
The goal is never to pressure — it's to remove genuine uncertainty. A prospect who says no because they're scared is not a lost lead. They're a future client if you handle it with patience and precision.

Retain, Extend, Scale
Path 01 · Primary
Extend 3–6 Months
Week 8–10 re-sign conversation. Review results together, present what's still possible, and propose the extension naturally.
"You've made real progress. Here's what I think the next 3 months could unlock — want to keep going?"

Clients who can't renew at full price are offered a mid-ticket program or alumni community. Keeps them in the ecosystem. Keeps the relationship alive.

Build the Business, Not Just the Practice

Most operators discover their capacity ceiling by hitting it. Define it before you're overwhelmed — and plan the hires before you need them.

1–10
clients
Solo Operator
You handle all delivery, sales, and admin. This is sustainable if delivery per client is tight and systems are in place.
Hire when: DMs exceed 2 hrs/day or MRR hits $8–10k
10–25
clients
Hire a Setter
Setter handles inbound DMs, lead qualification, and call booking. You focus on closing and delivery only.
Hire when: lead volume is high but close rate is dropping due to slow response
25–50
clients
Hire a Support Coach
Support coach handles a portion of delivery calls and async support. You step into the CEO role — strategy, top clients, and team management.
Hire when: delivery exceeds 4 hrs/day or MRR consistently above $20k
